Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to file my taxes?

You'll typically need:

  • W-2 forms from all employers
  • 1099 forms for other income (interest, dividends, freelance work)
  • Social Security cards for you and your dependents
  • Bank account information for direct deposit
  • Prior year tax return for reference
  • Receipts for deductible expenses (if itemizing)
How do I know if I should itemize deductions?

You should itemize deductions if your total itemized deductions exceed the standard deduction amount. For 2024, the standard deduction amounts are:

  • Single: $13,850
  • Married Filing Jointly: $27,700
  • Married Filing Separately: $13,850
  • Head of Household: $20,800

Common itemized deductions include mortgage interest, state and local taxes, charitable contributions, and medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of your adjusted gross income.

When is the tax filing deadline?

The tax filing deadline for 2024 tax returns is typically April 15, 2025. If this date falls on a weekend or holiday, the deadline is extended to the next business day.

You can request an automatic 6-month extension by filing Form 4868, which extends the filing deadline to October 15, 2025. However, this is an extension to file, not to pay - any taxes owed are still due by the original deadline.

How secure is my personal information?

TaxEasy takes your privacy and security seriously. We use:

  • 256-bit SSL encryption for all data transmission
  • Multi-factor authentication for account access
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Secure data centers with 24/7 monitoring
  • Compliance with IRS security standards

Your data is never shared with third parties without your explicit consent.

Can I save my progress and return later?

Yes! TaxEasy automatically saves your progress as you work on your tax return. You can log out at any time and return to complete your filing. Your data is securely stored in your account and will be available whenever you log back in.

We recommend saving frequently and keeping a backup of important documents on your local device as well.

What if I make a mistake on my tax return?

If you discover an error after filing, you can file an amended return using Form 1040-X. Common reasons for amendments include:

  • Missing income or deductions
  • Incorrect filing status
  • Mathematical errors
  • Changes to dependents claimed

You generally have three years from the original filing deadline to file an amended return.
